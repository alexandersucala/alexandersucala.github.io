About

Alexander designs and builds production AI systems where failure means silence, not hallucination. His architectural approach treats large language models as probabilistic components embedded within deterministic control structures, never as autonomous decision-makers. Every system he ships enforces fail-closed execution, hallucination verification against ground truth, and confidence provenance classification that distinguishes document-backed findings from model opinion.

His patent portfolio spans fail-closed execution gates, deterministic-first AI triggering, constrained self-correction schemas, bidirectional decision quality analysis, four-quadrant diagnostic regime detection, hallucination verification, confidence provenance classification, artifact-based authority, zero-loop control, and corpus-governed novelty checking. These are not theoretical frameworks. Each one is implemented in at least one production system.

He also leads the Alexandrian Unified Field Geometry (AUFG) program, an independent, falsification-first research effort in geometry-driven field computation with its own patent and publication track.
